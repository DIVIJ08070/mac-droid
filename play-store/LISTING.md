## Full description (max 4000 chars)
```
Bifrost is the bridge between your Mac and your Android phone. Two apps, one encrypted link — everything flows straight between your own devices over your own network. No cloud. No account. No subscription.

Built for the gap Apple and Google leave open: a MacBook that finally talks to an Android phone.

— WHAT IT DOES —

• Universal clipboard — copy on one device, paste on the other. Text and images, both directions.

• File & photo transfer — drag files onto your Mac, or share to Bifrost from any Android app. Browse your phone's gallery and files right on the Mac.

• Sync Folder — a folder that mirrors both ways automatically, like a private Dropbox that never touches a server.

• Phone as a Mac microphone — stream your phone's mic to your Mac for calls and recordings.

• Mac audio on your phone — play your Mac's sound through the phone's speaker or its Bluetooth headset.

• Screen mirroring, both ways — see and control your phone on your Mac, or mirror your Mac to your phone.

• Remote control & trackpad — use your phone as a full trackpad for your Mac, with gestures.

• Notifications on your Mac — your phone's notifications appear on the Mac, with action buttons and inline reply.

• Phone battery in the Mac menu bar, media controls, browser-tab handoff, and more.

— PRIVATE BY DESIGN —

Bifrost has no servers and no account. Your devices connect directly to each other and every transfer is end-to-end encrypted (ECDH P-256 + AES-256-GCM). Nothing leaves your devices — we literally can't see any of it.

— CONNECT ANY WAY —

Same Wi-Fi, your phone's hotspot, a USB cable, or remotely through a private VPN. Whatever path exists between your two devices, Bifrost uses it.

— FREE & OPEN —

Bifrost is free and open source. Pair once with a 6-digit code; after that it reconnects on its own.

Requires the free Bifrost companion app on your Mac (macOS 13+), available at the website.
```
