BIFROST

 
 

Built for the gap between macOS and Android. Bifrost continuously carries your clipboard, files, audio and input across both devices as a single adaptive connection layer — Wi-Fi, hotspot, USB or remote.

 
 

Why Bifrost

Your Mac and Android,
finally acting as one.

Free, private, and no cloud. Bifrost carries your clipboard, files, screen, audio and input between the two — over Wi-Fi, hotspot, USB, or across the world.

Connect four ways

Whatever you have, Bifrost links up:

📶 Same Wi-Fi 🔥 Phone hotspot 🔌 USB cable 🌍 Tailscale remote

Works anywhere

No Wi-Fi around? Use the phone's hotspot or a cable. Far away? Reach your Mac from cellular, a café, another city — encrypted end-to-end, no cloud in the middle.

Phone as a desktop

Run your Android in a resizable desktop window on your Mac — Samsung DeX-style, but with any Android phone, not just Samsung. Your phone stays free.

Copy here, paste there

Copy text or an image on your phone and ⌘V it straight into any Mac app. And the other way. One shared clipboard across both devices.

…plus file transfer, a full trackpad, screen mirroring both ways, audio streaming, notification mirroring, tab handoff and more. See everything ↓

Desktop
Desktop Mode BETA

Your phone,
as a desktop.

Open a full Android desktop in its own window on your Mac. Apps run on a separate virtual screen — so your actual phone stays free to use. Drive it with your mouse and keyboard, resize the window, work like it's a computer.

Separate screen

Apps open on a virtual display, not a mirror — your phone screen stays yours.

Mouse & keyboard

Point, click and type like a real desktop. Resize the window to any size.

One click

Launch it straight from the Bifrost app — it reconnects your phone automatically.

ⓘ Requires Wireless debugging on the phone — Settings → Developer options → Wireless debugging. It's the only way Android lets an app host a desktop, so this one needs developer mode on.
⌨️ Play games with keyboard & mouse — advanced, optional

For mapping keys/mouse to touch controls (e.g. shooters), use the free open-source tool QtScrcpy — it has a low-latency key-mapper built for games.

⚠️ Use at your own risk. Mapping keyboard/mouse to a mobile game may violate that game's rules and get your account banned — check the game's Terms of Service first.

Setup Guide

Two minutes.
Then it's automatic.

Four ways to connect

📶

Same Wi-Fi

Both on your home or office network. Automatic discovery.

🔥

Phone hotspot

No Wi-Fi around? The Mac joins your phone's hotspot — works anywhere, uses no data.

🔌

USB cable

Zero network needed. Click "Set up the USB link" on the Mac, then Connect by address → 127.0.0.1 on the phone.

🌍

Anywhere

With free Tailscale on both devices: Connect by address using your Mac's Tailscale IP.

01

Install on the Mac

Fastest way — paste this in Terminal. It installs straight to Applications and opens with no security warning:

Prefer a normal download? Get the zip here — unzip, move Bifrost.app to Applications, open it once — macOS will say it "could not verify" the app (it isn't Apple-notarized). Click Done, then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click "Open Anyway". One time only.

02

Install on the phone

Download the APK and tap it. Allow "install unknown apps" when Android asks — normal for anything outside the Play Store. If Play Protect says "app blocked": tap More details → Install anyway, or briefly toggle off scanning in Play Store → profile → Play Protect → settings, install, then toggle it back on. It triggers because Bifrost uses mic/screen/accessibility for its features and isn't on the Play Store.

03

Pair

Connect the devices any of the four ways above, both apps open. Tap your Mac on the phone, check the 6-digit code matches, click Accept on the Mac. That's the last code you'll ever type.

04

Grant as you go

Each feature asks for its permission on first use — touchpad wants Accessibility, audio wants Screen & System Audio Recording, tab sync wants Automation. Grant once, forget forever.
