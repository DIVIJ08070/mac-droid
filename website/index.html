MACDROID

 
 

Built for the gap between macOS and Android. MacDroid continuously carries your clipboard, files, audio and input across both devices as a single adaptive connection layer — on your own Wi-Fi.

 
 

A connection layer built on your own local network. MacDroid turns two separate devices into one continuous machine. Every copy becomes a shared clipboard. Every file arrives before you look up. Every gesture on glass moves a cursor on the desk. No cloud. No account. No wires.

Setup Guide

Two minutes.
Then it's automatic.

01

Install on the Mac

Fastest way — paste this in Terminal. It installs straight to Applications and opens with no security warning:

Prefer a normal download? Get the zip here — unzip, move MacDroid.app to Applications, open it once — macOS will say it "could not verify" the app (it isn't Apple-notarized). Click Done, then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click "Open Anyway". One time only.

02

Install on the phone

Download the APK and tap it. Allow "install unknown apps" when Android asks — normal for anything outside the Play Store.

03

Pair

Same Wi-Fi, both apps open. Tap your Mac on the phone, check the 6-digit code matches, click Accept on the Mac. That's the last code you'll ever type.

04

Grant as you go

Each feature asks for its permission on first use — touchpad wants Accessibility, audio wants Screen & System Audio Recording, tab sync wants Automation. Grant once, forget forever.
